## O1-B Evidence Criteria

Need **3 or more** of these:

1. **Leading/Starring Role**: Major role in production with distinguished reputation
2. **Critical Acclaim**: Published reviews, articles about your work
3. **Organizations**: Work with distinguished/notable organizations
4. **Commercial Success**: Box office, streaming, revenue metrics
5. **Recognition**: Awards, honors from industry experts
6. **High Compensation**: Salary significantly above field average

## Evidence Quality Framework

**Strong Evidence**:
- Directly demonstrates extraordinary ability
- From credible, verifiable sources
- Quantifiable impact or recognition
- Recent and relevant

**Medium Evidence**:
- Supports claim but less direct
- From less prominent sources
- Harder to quantify
- May need additional context

**Weak Evidence**:
- Tangential to extraordinary ability
- Difficult to verify
- Limited impact demonstration
- Consider if worth including

## Letter of Recommendation Guidelines

**Ideal Letter Writer**:
- Recognized expert in your field
- Has direct knowledge of your work
- Can speak to specific achievements
- Willing to write detailed letter (2-3 pages)

**Letter Should Include**:
1. Writer's credentials and qualifications
2. Relationship to you and your work
3. Specific examples of extraordinary ability
4. Comparison to peers in the field
5. Impact of your work on the industry
6. Conclusion about extraordinary ability status

## Filing Timeline Considerations

**Typical Timeline**:
- Evidence gathering: 2-4 weeks
- Letter collection: 4-6 weeks
- Petition assembly: 2-3 weeks
- Attorney review: 1-2 weeks
- USCIS processing: 2-3 months (standard)
- Premium processing: 15 days (additional fee)

**Critical Path Items**:
1. Peer consultation letter (can take 4-6 weeks)
2. Letters of recommendation (allow 6-8 weeks)
3. US employer/agent contract (timeline varies)
